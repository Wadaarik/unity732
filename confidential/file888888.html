        History :

        The KGB or Комите́т Госуда́рственной Безопа́сности, Komitet Gosudarstvennoy Bezopasnosti
        as Committee for State Security, was the main security agency for
        the Soviet Union from 1954
        The agency is a military service governed by army laws and regulations,
        in the same fashion as the Soviet Army

        Chronology :

        1917–1922	Cheka under SNK of the RSFSR
        1922–1923	GPU under NKVD of the RSFSR
        1923–1934	OGPU under SNK of the USSR
        1934–1941	NKVD of the USSR
        1941███████     MGB of the USSR

        1943–1946	NKGB of the USSR
        1946–1953	MGB of the USSR
        1953–1954	MVD of the USSR
        1954–1978	KGB under SM of the USSR

        The GRU (military intelligence) recruited the ideological
        agent Julian Wadleigh, who became a State Department diplomat
        in 1936. The NKVD's first US operation was establishing the legal
        residency of Boris Bazarov and the illegal residency of
        Iskhak Akhmerov in 1934. Throughout, the ████████████ (CPUSA) and its
        General Secretary Earl Browder, helped NKVD recruit Americans,
        working in government, business, and industry.[citation needed]

        Other important, low-level and high-level ideological agents were the diplomats
        ████████████████████ and Michael Whitney Straight in the State Department,
        the statistician Harry Dexter White in the Treasury Department,
        the economist Lauchlin Currie (an FDR advisor), and the "Silvermaster Group",
        headed by statistician Greg Silvermaster, in the Farm Security Administration
        and the Board of Economic Warfare. Moreover, when Whittaker Chambers, formerly
        Alger Hiss's courier, approached the Roosevelt Government—to identify the
        Soviet spies Duggan, White, and others—he was ignored.
        Hence, during the Second World War (1939–45)—at the Tehran (1943), Yalta (1945),
